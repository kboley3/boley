Faint Galaxy Detection

My previous work involved the detection of the counterimages of a high redshift (z~10) galaxy candidate that is spatially resolved in Hubble Space Telescope (HST) imaging of galaxy cluster. During this research, I developed a program called REGAL that conducts blind searches for counter images of sheared galaxies around galaxy clusters using a maximum likelihood detection method.

Planet Formation

Currently, I work with Dr. Ji Wang on planet occurrence as it relates to metallicity and its implications for planet formation. Jovian planet occurrence has a strong correlation with metallicity, which may act as a tracer for planet formation. However, planet occurrence has also been tied to other properties such as alpha element abundance and velocities. We are working to determine the strongest tracers for planet formation so that we can determine when and where planets form in our galaxy.

Hot Jupiter Planet Occurrence

I investigated hot Jupiter planet occurrence in the metal-poor regime (-2 < [Fe/H] < -0.6). By considering the most metal-poor stars, we can glimpse into the past to understand planet formation and what role metallicity plays in planet formation. It is an important first step in determining how planet formation occurs on a larger scale. This work serves as an archor point for future work where I will constrain the functional form of Jovian planet occurrence vs metallicity.
